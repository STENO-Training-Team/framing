Framing Expectations Guide

🎯 Pre-Record Standards
  • Address ALL framing issues before going on the record
  • Witness centered with head and shoulders visible
  • Face clearly lit without shadows or backlighting
  • Background professional (plain or blurred)
  • Landscape mode if joining from phone
  • Get Taking Attorney approval if perfect framing can't be achieved
⏱️ During the Deposition
  • Monitor witness framing throughout the deposition
  • Wait for a break to address any changes
  • Only interrupt during a lull, NOT during active questioning
  • Document framing issues on Video Worksheet with timestamps
  • Note any witness disconnections with details
💬 When to Defer to Counsel
  • Witness becomes resistant or agitated about framing requests
  • You've exhausted all reasonable adjustment requests
  • Ask TA: "Are you happy with the current framing?"
  • If TA approves current setup, proceed without further requests
  • Document any framing limitations on your worksheet
📱 Special Situations
  • Phone users: Request landscape mode and stable surface placement
  • Shared camera (attorney + witness): Center witness; ask if attorney has another device
  • Owl/split-screen cameras: Request single-view camera; if unavoidable, document on worksheet and contact #video-help for guidance
  • Virtual backgrounds: Request blur filter or removal
💡

Pro Tip: When you see GOOD framing, acknowledge it! Let the witness know they're doing it right and ask them to maintain that positioning throughout the deposition.

Quick Coaching Phrases

For Good Framing (Acknowledge it!): "Your current video feed image is clear and well-centered, so please be mindful of maintaining this positioning throughout the deposition."
For General Adjustments: "Since this deposition is being recorded, I want to get your framing set up correctly. Could you please center your face in the frame with enough room above and below your head?"
For Dark Lighting: "It seems that the current lighting in the room is a bit dim. Would it be possible for you to brighten up the space by turning on a light or opening a window in the direction you're facing?"
For Bright/Backlit Issues: "It appears that the room you're in is quite bright. To achieve better framing, would you be able to adjust the lighting by closing blinds or curtains, or moving to a slightly different spot?"
For Portrait Mode (Phone): "For optimal framing, could you rotate your phone to landscape mode? If you need assistance changing your orientation, I can help walk you through the process."
For Virtual Backgrounds: "It seems that the background behind you is somewhat distracting. Would it be possible to enable the 'blur' background in Zoom or choose a different location?"
For Off-Center Positioning: "It seems that your current positioning might need some adjustment for better framing. Would it be possible for you to recenter yourself within the frame, ensuring that you are well-positioned and aligned?"
